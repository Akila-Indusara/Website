John Wick


     John Wick is a 2014 American action thriller film directed by Chad Stahelski and written by Derek Kolstad. The film follows John Wick (Keanu Reeves), a legendary hitman who is forced out of retirement to seek revenge against the men who killed his puppy, a final gift from his recently deceased wife. John Wick also stars Michael Nyqvist, Alfie Allen, Adrianne Palicki, Bridget Moynahan, Dean Winters, Ian McShane, John Leguizamo, and Willem Dafoe.
  • Directed by -
    Chad Stahelski
         Chad Stahelski is an American stuntman and film director. He directed the 2014 film John Wick and its three sequels. He has worked as a stuntman, stunt coordinator and second unit director on several films.He worked as a stunt double for his future John Wick star Keanu Reeves in The Matrix franchise, and later served as a stunt co-ordinator for the films. In 2009, Stahelski was second-unit director and stunt co-ordinator on Ninja Assassin along with David Leitch.
  • Produced by -
    Basil Iwanyk
         Basil William Iwanyk is an American film producer. He is the founder of Thunder Road Films and most known for Sicario, The Town, Greenland and the John Wick franchise. His films have earned more than $2 billion internationally as of 2018. He is of Ukrainian descent.After starting out working for Warner Bros in the 1990s, Basil founded his own company, Thunder Road, in 2000. The company has a stellar reputation within the industry and a remarkable track record of critical and commercial success

Summit officially launched in 1993 by Patrick Wachsberger, Bob Hayward, and David Garrett under the name Summit Entertainment LP as a distribution and sales organization. The plot of John Wick


     The story revolves around the titular character, John Wick, a former assassin who is mourning the death of his beloved wife, Helen. After her death, she leaves John a puppy named Daisy as a final gift to help him cope with his grief.
     However, John's life takes a dark turn when he encounters a group of Russian gangsters led by Iosef Tarasov. Iosef takes an interest in John's vintage car and decides to steal it. In the process, he and his thugs break into John's home, beat him up, and kill Daisy. Unbeknownst to Iosef, John Wick is a legendary and lethal assassin with a fearsome reputation.
     Devastated by the loss of his wife's final gift and enraged by the senseless killing of Daisy, John Wick decides to come out of retirement to seek vengeance. He reconnects with his old friend and fellow assassin, Marcus, who offers some help. John is determined to exact retribution on those responsible, even though it means going up against his former employer, Viggo Tarasov, Iosef's father and a powerful crime lord.
     As John Wick embarks on his mission of revenge, he leaves a trail of destruction in his wake. His lethal skills, expert combat techniques, and relentless pursuit make him a force to be reckoned with. Viggo, realizing the storm that John is unleashing, attempts to protect his son by sending a succession of highly skilled assassins after him.
     Throughout the film, John encounters various adversaries, showcasing his deadly efficiency in combat and resourcefulness. He also discovers that there is a code among assassins, and certain locations, like the Continental Hotel, serve as neutral territories where killing is strictly prohibited.
     As the body count rises, John's actions attract the attention of the criminal underworld, and a bounty is placed on his head. He faces formidable challenges, but his determination and skills are unwavering.
     "John Wick" is known for its high-octane action sequences, stylish visuals, and Keanu Reeves' compelling performance as the enigmatic and lethal assassin. The film's success led to several sequels that further explore the world of John Wick and his dangerous adventures in the criminal underworld.

Behind The scenes


     Stunt Team and Choreography: The films are renowned for their meticulously choreographed action sequences. The expert stunt team, led by Chad Stahelski (the film's director, a former stuntman himself), worked tirelessly to create visually stunning and realistic fight scenes.
     The franchise popularized a shooting style called "Gun Fu," which blends martial arts movements with gunplay. The choreography and execution of these sequences made them stand out and garnered a dedicated fan following.
     The filmmakers emphasized practical effects over CGI whenever possible, resulting in more visceral and impactful action scenes. This approach added to the gritty and grounded feel of the films.
     The films' cinematography, led by Dan Laustsen, utilized vibrant colors, creative angles, and long takes to capture the intensity of the action while maintaining a sleek and stylish visual aesthetic. International Locations: The films were shot in various global locations, contributing to their international appeal and providing diverse and visually striking settings.
     The first "John Wick" film gained a strong following upon its release, leading to the creation of sequels that maintained the same high standard of action and storytelling.

DID YOU KNOW?


    The car John Wick drives in the movie is a 1969 Ford Mustang Mach 1, a vintage and iconic muscle car. Unfortunately, it meets a tragic fate early in the film when it is stolen by the Russian gangsters.
     Also the gold coins used as currency in the assassin underworld were created specifically for the film and have become a recognizable symbol associated with the franchise.
     The filmmakers strived to use practical effects whenever possible, minimizing the reliance on CGI to maintain a more grounded and visceral feel to the action. Inspired by Real Events: The character of John Wick and the story were partly inspired by real-life events. Director Chad Stahelski mentioned that some aspects of the film were inspired by stories of retired military and police personnel seeking justice.
